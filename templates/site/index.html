Diagnostic Framework

The Four Failure Modes

After decades of project oversight, we've found that every troubled project traces back to one or more of these root causes. Identifying which ones are at play is the first step toward recovery.

01

Bad Estimate at Origin

The project was underfunded from the start. Whether driven by optimism bias, competitive pressure, or incomplete scope definition, the budget never reflected reality. Every downstream decision is distorted.

02

Inefficient Operations

The plan may have been sound, but execution is burning money. Poor sequencing, inadequate supervision, rework, and labor inefficiency compound daily. The schedule says one thing; the field says another.

03

Failure to Capture Change

Scope changed, but the contract didn't. Change orders are undocumented, under-priced, or unapproved. The contractor absorbs cost until they can't — then everyone pays.

04

Conflict of Agency

Someone at the table is optimizing for themselves, not the project. Misaligned incentives between owner, contractor, CM, and designer create decisions that look rational individually but damage the whole.
